Project Overview

In todayâ€™s busy world, managing finances can be overwhelming. Users face challenges with tracking balances, managing transactions, and making payments conveniently and securely. Getting Started

Designing Bhachat, a user-friendly e-wallet app, began with research and user insights to understand key needs like security, finance management and seamless transactions. I started by creating wireframes and prototypes in Figma, ensuring an intuitive and accessible user experience. With a focus on clean design and smooth navigation, I incorporated essential features such as two-factor authentication, quick fund transfers, and budget tracking. Through usability testing and iteration, I refined the design to enhance usability and efficiency, making Bhachat a reliable and secure financial management solution.

Qualitative Research

Interview Questions

  • To identify the key features and functionalities desired by users in an e-wallet app to enhance convenience and ease of use.
  • To evaluate user perceptions and concerns regarding security measures in financial applications.
  • To understand specific scenarios where users struggle with bill splitting, fund transfers, and expense tracking.
  • To determine which functionalities, such as bill-splitting, budget setting, or security measures like two-factor authentication, are most valued by users.
  • To identify user preferences regarding interface design, ease of use, and overall userfriendliness.

Key Insight Derived

  • Security emerged as a top priority for all participants.
  • Tracking expenses accurately was also cited as a common challenge.
  • Two-factor authentication was seen as crucial for enhanced security, influencing users' app preferences.
  • Ease of use and user-friendliness is most important for user satisfaction.
  • Needs clearer information regarding e-wallet app security practices.

Quantitive Research

I did surveys on 25 participants. Below are the results:

Key Insight Derived

  • Nearly all participants (92%) consider quick transfer feature crucial, highlighting the importance of speed and efficiency in transactions.
  • High importance is placed on security features.
  • Minimalistic and Clean Design is preferred by participants, this design approach is favored for its simplicity and ease of use.
  • Expense Categorization feature is less critical compared to fund transfers and security but still hold significant value.

Information Architecture & Card Sorting

I conducted a closed card sorting exercise for our e-wallet app with a specific focus on five core categories: Send Money, Request Money, History, Budgeting, Settings. There were total of 5 participants. The results revealed clear user associations, leading to adjustments in the sitemap to better align with user expectations and improve the overall user flow.

Usability Testing

Once the Bhachat prototype was complete, I conducted usability testing to identify and address any user experience issues. I developed a test plan and recruited 8 participants to perform 5 specific tasks. Using affinity mapping and a rainbow spreadsheet, I analyzed the results to pinpoint usability problems and gather valuable feedback. This process allowed me to refine the prototype and enhance the overall design.

Ideas & Solutions

Issue 1

The user interface does not provide visual indicators for progress or achievements, leaving users without immediate feedback on their status or accomplishments.

Solution 1

I added progress bars and achievement markers to provide users with immediate feedback on their status, enhancing clarity and overall satisfaction.

Issue 2

Users noted that the "Signup" text doesnâ€™t look clickable. It needs underlining and a contrasting color, as should "Login" and "Resend," to make them clearly identifiable as links.

Solution 2

I styled the "Signup" text as a link by adding an underline and applied the same styling to "Login" and "Resend" to ensure they are clearly identifiable as clickable links.

Issue 3

Users have reported that the values on top of the bars are missing, making it difficult to interpret the data. This lack of labels on the bars reduces clarity and hinders users' ability to easily understand the information being presented.

Solution 3

I added labels displaying the values on top of the bars to enhance clarity. This change ensures that each bar now has a value displayed, making it easier for users to understand the data.
