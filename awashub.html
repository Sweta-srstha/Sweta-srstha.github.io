Awashub

Awashub is a real estate app for buying, selling, and renting properties. It offers advanced search filters, agent connections, and a seamless user experience across devices.

Industry

PropTech | B2C

Role

UI / UX Design

Team

Solo Project

Duration

8 weeks

Approach

User-centered Design

Tools

Figma, Figjam, Slack

Challenge

The property search process can be overwhelming due to fragmented listings, inefficient filters, and poor mobile usability. Users needed a seamless and intuitive platform to browse properties, connect with agents, and save their preferences efficiently.

Solution

I designed Awashub with a responsive interface, advanced search filters, and an optimized user experience. The app enables users to effortlessly find, compare, and connect with property owners or agents, ensuring a smooth and stress-free real estate journey.

Result

Users can easily buy, sell, and rent properties with Awashub's intuitive design, advanced filters, and responsive interface.

Project Overview

Awashub is a responsive real estate app I designed during my UX Bootcamp at CareerFoundry. It simplifies buying, selling, and renting properties with advanced search filters, seamless navigation, and a user-friendly experience across devices.

Getting Started

The product is a concept project, with UX research and personas already defined. My focus was on UI design, and the next step was creating a user flow that ensures easy, intuitive navigation for a seamless user experience. By outlining the user flow, I ensured that each step of the journey was logically connected, enhancing usability and guiding users through the appâ€™s core features in a seamless, efficient manner.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

I started with low-fidelity wireframes to establish the basic layout and functionality of the app. This approach helped me focus on user flow and core interactions, allowing for quick adjustments to enhance the user experience before moving into more detailed design work.

Moodboard

I created a modern, minimalist mood board for the app to capture a clean and user-focused aesthetic. The goal was to design an interface that feels elegant and intuitive, allowing users to navigate smoothly without distraction. This style keeps the app user-friendly and visually uncluttered, enhancing the experience of browsing properties effortlessly.

After establishing the moodboard, I moved into creating the style guide, which provided clear guidelines for the app's visual consistency. This step focused on defining the design elements to ensure a cohesive look throughout the app.

Design for different breakpoints

I began by designing for mobile, prioritizing a clean and intuitive layout for smaller screens. Once the mobile design was finalized, I adapted it for tablet and desktop breakpoints. For tablets, I adjusted spacing and element sizes to optimize touch interactions while maintaining clarity. For desktops, I utilized the additional screen real estate to enhance content display and navigation, ensuring consistency and usability across all devices.

Final Design

Takeaways

* Mobile-First Design Approach:

Designing for mobile first taught me to focus on the essentials, ensuring clarity and functionality in smaller spaces before scaling up for larger screens.

* Responsive Design Mastery:

Creating designs for multiple breakpoints helped me understand how to adapt layouts and elements for various devices, maintaining consistency and usability.

* User-Centered Iteration:

Continuously testing and refining designs based on user feedback helped me develop a mindset of iteration, ensuring the final product effectively meets user expectations.

* UI Fundamentals in Practice:

Applying principles like typography, color theory, and spacing gave me hands-on experience in creating visually appealing and user-friendly interfaces.

* Problem-Solving Through Design:

Working on the real estate app emphasized the importance of aligning design decisions with user needs, translating research into practical solutions.

* Accessibility Awareness:

Designing with inclusivity in mind reinforced the importance of contrast, readability, and navigation, making the interface usable for a diverse range of users.

Next Steps

Next, I plan to refine the visual design to make it more polished and consistent. I will work on creating high-fidelity prototypes to showcase interactions and ensure the user journey feels smooth. I also want to expand the designs for more breakpoints and conduct usability testing to gather feedback and make further improvements based on real user insights.
